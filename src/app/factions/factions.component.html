The Ishimatsu Clan

TODO: text about the group

  • Play Style:

    Well-Rounded

  • Cultural Perks:

    Unyielding Will: All 1’s are rerolled in combat regardless of whether the Ishimatsu Clan is attacking or defending

  • Special Unit: Ishimatsu Daimyo

    A single specialty unit that when placed on a territory increases the range of its prosperity by one in both directions, not including the original prosperity number. In this way resources become easier to gather in that specific territory. For example, if placed on a prosperity 7 token the daimyo will allow that territory to gather resources on 6’s and 8’s. In all other respects the Daimyo behaves as a regular Ishimatsu unit.

TODO: text about the group

  • Play Style:

    Defensive

  • Cultural Perks:

    Protector of the People: When occupying a territory with an unbuilt wall the Kingdom of Beschützer can build completed wooden walls at no cost during their construction phase

    Experienced Masons: Stone walls cost half as much

  • Special Unit: Beschützer Knights

    Each Beschützer knight present in a defending army adds one specialty die (up to two) to each clash within the battle. The knights cannot be destroyed by an enemy die unless all other basic defending units have been killed. For every basic defensive die you may also roll a specialty knight die unless you only have a single unit left in which case two knight die can be used with only one basic defense die. The special knight die can be swapped for any regular die to the purpose of aiding the defender. Losses only result in the death of basic units and not knights unless all basic units have already been killed.

The Skallen Raiders

TODO: text about the group

  • Play Style:

    Offensive

  • Cultural Perks:

    Pillage & Plunder: after destroying an enemy village the Skallen raiders receive twice that villages gold income immediately as loot and all the resources required to construct it (4gold). The same goes for cities (8gold) and metropolises (12gold)

    Relentless Raiders: When attacking garrisons the Skallen raiders force the enemy to reroll all 6’s

    Supply Sappers: For every territory that is taken from an enemy player the Skallen raiders may steal one random resource from that players hand

  • Special Unit: Skallen Gjallarhorns

    Once per offensive clash (even if there is more than one Gjallarhorn present) the Skallen Gjallarhorn can attempt to inspire their allies by selecting and rerolling one allied die. Besides this perk they behave as basic units within combat.

The Chrysós Syndicate:

TODO: text about the group

  • Play Style:

    Diplomatic

  • Cultural Perks:

    Masters of the Sea: The Chrysós Syndicate can always utilize water routes regardless of chaos events

    Trade Networks: For the Chrysós Syndicate resources can be purchased for three gold as opposed to four. Units can also be purchased for seven gold as opposed to eight

    Boundless Profit: The Chrysós Syndicate can trade with any player even if not connected via adjacent territories

  • Special Unit: Chrysós Naval Fleets

    Chrysós naval fleets can be purchased and placed on any water route. Naval fleets are composed of four individual ships that behave in combat as mobile ‘stone walls’ (All four dice are rolled and the boats ALWAYS win ties). To replenish a naval fleet a new fleet must be constructed in its place. A single fleet can be moved to another water route every turn and can only be constructed if the Chrysós Syndicate controls a territory connected to the sea (not a lake). Only four Chrysós naval fleets can be on the map at a time. The Chrysós Syndicate can allow enemy units to pass through these naval fleets and barter for the price of that ability for their own benefit

The Khünik Horde

TODO: text about the group

  • Play Style:

    Mobile

  • Cultural Perks:

    Horse Masters: During the fortification phase of the turn order the Khünik Horde may reinforce any number of Khunik Riders to any territory, which they control in unbroken succession (this cannot be done over water routes)

    Nomadic Peoples: As a nomadic people Khünik settlements are always easily moved. The Khünik Horde cannot construct cities or metropolises, but can build up to 15 villages. These villages can be moved across the map by one space per turn and up to two can gather resources from the same territory. When attacked each village wields its single garrison of one unit separately. They can move to any discovered location during the exploration phase with no other enemy units.

  • Special Unit: Khünik Riders

    Khünik riders are constructed at a lower cost than basic units, but all rolls above a 4 count only as a 4 in both offensive and defensive battles. For each Khünik rider present in an offensive battle an additional special die may be added (up to three) for a maximum of six offensive die per clash.

The Equani Tribe

TODO: text about the group

  • Play Style:

    Objective Based

  • Cultural Perks:

    Sacrificial Worship: Can sacrifice one basic unit for three faith

    Conscription of the Gods: Can sacrifice three faith for one basic unit

    Tribal Worship: Temples can be constructed with any combination of wood and stone

  • Special Unit: Equani Shaman

    When placed on a territory with a constructed temple the Equani Shaman allows the tribe to passively decrease the activation cost of the occupied temple (by two faith per shaman). Having an excess of Equani Shaman occupying a temple has no effect beyond allowing the perk to be activated for free (Equani Shaman have no effect on temples that do not cost faith to be activated). In combat the Equani Shaman acts as a normal unit and does not provide any specific battle advantage. However, if a shaman is present in an attacking or defending army each allied unit who perishes will grant the Equani Tribe one faith.
