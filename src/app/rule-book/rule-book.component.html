The Rules of Imperium

Context :)

In the game of Imperium you will play as one of six factions vying for domination over a frontier of massive islands known together as the continent of “Trӧm”. Trӧm has for 100’s of years been a site of trade and prosperity for the six factions of the land, but shortages in resources and the increased volatility of the natural world has put the factions at each other's throats. The peaceful coexistence they once knew melts away as they push to acquire scarce resources and ensure the survival of their faction. Take leadership of your chosen faction and utilize their unique position and cultural affinities to crush, starve, and out-smart your enemies in the battle for total imperium over the chaotic land of Trӧm.

Setting Up the Game
STEP 1:

All players roll two six sided dice. The player with the highest roll is allowed to choose their faction first and the player with the next highest roll chooses second and so on and so forth (for a look at each faction refer to pages 6-8).

STEP 2:

Now that all players have chosen their factions, place one village from every player faction on its corresponding capital (only do this for factions chosen by a player). The faction capitals are listed below: The Chrysós Syndicate: TODO The Ishimatsu Clan: TODO The Skallen Raiders: TODO The Equani Tribe: TODO The Khünik Horde: TODO The Kingdom of Beschützer: TODO

STEP 3:

Now that the starting point for each player has been confirmed the relative prosperity of each region must be decided. Every capital territory in addition to TODO (THE GOLD MINE) will receive an unhidden number 7 prosperity token to be inserted into the board. The rest of the tokens will be placed face down and shuffled on a flat surface. At this point one random token will be inserted upside down on each territory from east to west. From this point on players will only flip prosperity tokens for territories that they occupy.

STEP 4:

At this point the board is set for play and every player can receive their starting resources, units, and structures: 6 basic units to be placed on the capital territory 2 Wood 2 Livestock 2 Iron 2 Grain 2 Stone

STEP 5:

The player turn order will be determined in the first round by the highest roll of two 6 sided dice (highest roll chooses their own turn position first). For all subsequent rounds turn order will be determined by the player with the most units (player with the most units goes first). If there is a tie the players involved will roll two six sided dice and the player with the highest roll will choose their position. Faith can also be used to alter the turn order. All players who wish to use their own faith to determine the turn order can choose to secretly bet faith under a goblet at the end of their turn, which is then placed on the table in front of all players to be revealed at the end of the turn cycle. Once all bets have been revealed, the player who wagered the most faith is allowed to choose the order in which all players will take their turn. The faith that was used to make this bet is then discarded by all players who participated. From this point on each player will move through each phase of the player action order in whatever player sequence determined before the turn cycle (player turn number tokens are available to make this decision easy to remember).

World Order Process
STEP 1:

Four six sided dice will be rolled to determine “The Chaos of Trӧm” (an event that will occur before the next turn cycle begins, but does not occur before the first turn cycle). Once the chaos event has been determined, but before it occurs, players who participated in betting during the “bet faith” phase of turn order will reveal their bet to the other players. The player with the highest bet must then choose to alter the number of the chaos roll by one in either direction. Both options will be made clear to the player and once the winning player has chosen the chaos event will occur (All faith bet during the process is discarded regardless of the outcome).

STEP 2:

After the chaos event occurs the player turn order will be determined for the cycle. The player with the most units on the board will go first, and so on and so forth. The number of units on the board will be determined only after the turn cycle has ended, so turn order cannot be altered in the middle of a turn cycle. As usual, players may also reveal and compare any wagered faith to determine turn order, which would take precedence over the player with the most units

Resource Gathering

In order to gather resources from a territory you must have a presence there. If you have one or more units on a territory and the prosperity token for that territory is rolled during a resource roll you will receive one of the predetermined resource for that territory. If you were to construct a village on that territory you would receive two predetermined resources, and if you were to construct a city you would receive three of the predetermined resources for that territory. A metropolis would allow you to receive three predetermined resource cards in addition to one of your choice (four in total).

Gold

Gold is produced passively by settlements and via the gold mine in the XXX territory at the center of the map. Villages produce 2 gold, cities produce 4, and a metropolis produces 6. A village (without considering its own production of gold) would allow the gold mine to produce 8 gold, a city would allow it to produce 12 gold, and a metropolis would allow it to produce 16 gold on an associated resource roll. A simple occupation of the gold mine would produce only 4 gold on an associated resource roll. Players can use gold to purchase or sell resources and can also recruit units at a minor discount (gold conversion and trade descriptions listed below).

  • Purchase 1 resource = 4 gold
  • Sell 1 resource = 1 gold
  • Recruit one unit = 4 gold
Rules of Construction
In order to construct a building or wall you must first have a military presence in the territory.
  • Each territory can contain only contain only one village, city, or metropolis at a time (with the exception of Khünik villages)
  • In order to build a metropolis you must first build a city and in order to build a city you must first construct a village (these structures are upgraded and not built in addition to existing structures)
  • Temples and structures belonging to an enemy player are destroyed in the wake of any successful invasion of the territory in which they reside (temple perks are redrawn upon reconstruction)
  • When attacked, villages provides a garrison of one defense die to be used if all other units on the territory have been killed. A city provides a garrison of two defense die as opposed to one, and a metropolis provides a garrison of four defense die as opposed to two. If the structure isn’t destroyed all of the defense die will be available to the defending player for the next attack. Garrison defense die must all be rolled at the same time, which provides the metropolis with a defensive advantage of four defense die as opposed to the usual maximum of two.
  • Basic units can only be constructed in territories with a village, city, or metropolis
  • Specialty units can only be constructed in a city or metropolis

There are five pre-designated sacred sites on the continent that provide unique buffs for factions that construct their sacred buildings there. Though these are particularly potent for the Equani tribe, other factions can also expect great returns from these sacred site buildings. Each faction has a single unique building that can only be constructed on these sacred sites, though you could theoretically have five of these buildings if all sacred sites are controlled. Those buildings are as follows:

  • Beschützer Kingdom - Monastery:

    The monastery provides for the warriors of the Beschützer kingdom as part of their service to God. Any units stationed here do not require upkeep.

  • Ishimatsu Clan - Shinto Shrine:

    This shrine offers your people greater opportunity to connect with the land on which you live. You may re-roll your prosperity roll once per turn (this replaces the previous roll and is not an additional roll).

  • Skallen Raiders - Statue of Odin:

    Odin deserves tribute from every mortal, regardless of their willingness. Move any resource token on the map to this territory once per turn.

  • Chrysós Syndicate - Port of Poseidon:

    This magnificent center of trade increases the gold generated by all owned settlements (1 gold increase)

  • Khünik Horde - Palace of the Khan:

    A palace fitting of the wealth of the great Khan. Convert one of your mobile villages to a mobile city (if this city is destroyed you may upgrade an existing village for free so long as the sacred building still exists. If the sacred building is destroyed the city is automatically downgraded).

  • Equani Tribe - Temple of the Sun:

    For every temple that the Equani tribe owns they ascend a level according to the tiers listed below:

    • One Temple:

      Generate 8 faith

      Sacrificial Altar: Choose to either add one or subtract one from the chaos roll (you know each outcome)

    • Two Temple:

      Generate 16 faith

      Sacrificial Altar: Choose to either add one or subtract one from the chaos roll (you know each outcome)

      Virtue of the Faithful: Choose the turn order for players in the game

    • Three Temple:

Trade

Players can generally allow any connecting players to access their wealth, but there are a few minor exceptions to this process.

  • Players can only allow others to access their wealth if connected via unbroken territories (mountains disrupt this connection)
  • Wealth (gold and resources), DO NOT MOVE when trade is occurring. A player simply ‘opens their gates’ to allow another player access to a predetermined amount of wealth during a connected player's construction phase. There is no guarantee they will return the favor or hold up the terms of the deal during your construction phase, so tread carefully when it comes to your trade deals
  • Players cannot trade units, or structures
  • If access to wealth depends upon shared territory, passage through a wall, or a naval fleet. The access can be halted by the player who owns the wall or naval fleet. In cases where two players occupy a dependent territory, a dispute can break out to decide the outcome (only applies if no buildings are present on this shared territory)
Combat

All combat involving basic units is done in the same manner. The attacker will receive up to three dice that they will roll after announcing an attack from one territory to another. Each die must correspond to an available unit in the attacking territory. Once the attacker has rolled their three dice the defender will roll up to two defending dice. Both the attacking and defending dice are placed back to back so that the lowest numbers are on one side and the highest numbers are on the other. The highest roll of the attacker will be compared to the highest roll of the defender and the second highest roll of the attacker will be compared to the second highest roll of the defender and so on and so forth if applicable. For each defending die that is equal to or higher than an attacking die an attacking unit will perish. For each attacking die that is higher than a defending die a defending unit will perish (the defending player wins ties). This process will continue until the attacker either successfully defeats all of the defending units or decides to cease the attack. Specialty units add to this basic system of combat as described in the “Factions” portion of the rule book (Pg.6-8).

Walls

Walls, like settlements, act as a point for garrisons to take up arms without as much risk to the player. If an enemy decides to attack a wall the defending player must use a certain number of dice to defend for as many clashes as necessary until all of the defending dice have been defeated or the enemy has retreated. If the wall is defeated all of its pegs from that border are removed and the attacking player has the option to either continue into the territory or halt the attack in order to regroup. If the wall is not defeated, it regains any lost die from the previous battle for the next. Wooden walls receive two defense dice and stone walls receive four (All of the die must be rolled at the same time).

Walls are unique from settlement garrisons in that they also allow the defender to immediately reciprocate an attack (before the attacking player has an opportunity to refortify and move attacking units). If the attacker chooses to halt their attack at any point after sieging the wall and the wall remains, the besieged player may utilize any number of their troops from the adjacent territory behind the wall to launch a counter attack with offensive dice. During this battle the attacking player will win all ties, which affords a defensive advantage to an offensive battle. This perk can only be activated if the counter attacking player's wall was attacked by another player. This battle doesn’t allow the counter attacking player to occupy the territory immediately, but simply allows them to engage enemy units in a counter attack before retreating back to their defending territory.

Constructed walls will remain on borders no longer occupied by the player who owns the wall. These walls can be used to block or negotiate trade over a border. Enemy units can also be allowed to pass through the wall without combat, depending upon the discretion of the wall’s faction ruler.

The Fallen

The Fallen are a faction of undead that are unlikely to be introduced into any given game. If summoned there will undoubtedly be catastrophic consequences for all players. Fallen units behave exactly as all other basic units do with the exception that in combat any unit that dies to fallen warriors will be immediately converted to a basic fallen unit themselves. In this way the fallen can be extremely difficult to snuff out as defeated player units are constantly joining their ranks. Any villages under fallen control spawn one fallen unit in that territory during the chaos phase of the world order. Cities produce two and a metropolis produces three. The fallen are not affected by plague, but are affected by monsters in the Age of Shadow.

Chaos Rolls
DICE ROLL: (roll four dice)
  1. Hell Rising:
    Use the “Age of Oblivion” chaos rolls from this point on. The volcano at the center of the map launches molten rocks into the sky as the earth cracks and burns. The gold mine and every adjacent territory is burned to the ground and all units and buildings within those territories are corrupted by ‘The Fallen’...
  2. The Wrath of the Stars:
    A meteor strikes one territory at random. All structures and units on the territory are destroyed and the land becomes impassable for the remainder of the game.
  3. Disease Spreads:
    Two plague tokens are placed on two random territories (the plague immediately cuts the number of units in that territory in half).
  4. Dawn of Civilization:
    Upgrade all non-player controlled settlements by one level (the gold mine acquires a non-player controlled wooden wall whenever this is rolled, which destroys any player built walls)
  5. Stockpiles Burn:
    Destroy one resource token in every territory on the map
  6. Tables Turn:
    The player with the least number of occupied territories gets to swap two visible prosperity tokens on the board
  7. Spoils of the Weak:
    The player with the fewest units receives one gold for each unit
  8. Mines Collapse:
    No iron is generated this turn
  9. Treasuries Empty:
    No gold is generated this turn
  10. Crops Wither:
    No grain is generated this turn
  11. Strong Tides:
    No water routes may be used for travel
  12. Livestock Plagued:
    No livestock is generated this turn
  13. Wood Rots:
    No wood is generated this turn
  14. Quarries Collapse:
    No stone is generated this turn
  15. Unfortunate Tidings:
    Choose five random territories and remove all their wealth from the board
  16. Risks of Business:
    The territory with the most gold and the territory with the most resources must swap their tokens
  17. Treasuries Burn:
    Destroy two gold in every territory on the map
  18. Warriors Revolt:
    Players must pay double the usual upkeep for their units this turn
  19. Torrential Rains:
    Landslides reduce all buildings in territories that border mountains by one level.
  20. Waves of Destruction:
    Massive waves wash out all ocean facing territories. All buildings are reduced by one level and the number of occupying units are halved.
  21. Blinding Shadow:
    Use the “Age of Shadow” chaos rolls from this point on. All prosperity tokens including the ones on capital territories are removed, reshuffled and replaced (only occupied territories will be revealed and the fog of prosperity will be restored)...
Age of Shadow Chaos Rolls
DICE ROLL: (roll two dice)
  1. Apocalypse:
    All players lose the game and Trӧm is engulfed in shadow.
  2. Shadow Rulers:
    All players swap seats with the player to their right taking control of that player's faction (dead factions included in this process so that defeated players may re-enter the game).
  3. Something in the Darkness:
    For the rest of the game whenever a player attempts to cross a border (invasion or fortification) where a wall could be constructed the unit must roll a six sided die. If they roll a 4, 5, or 6 the unit has survived the journey and if they do not the unit will be consumed (the Ishimatsu Clan can reroll 1’s in this process). Everytime this chaos number is rolled all walls will be destroyed.
  4. Unexpected Sabotage:
    Each player is forced to wipe all structures (walls, settlements, and temples) from a single chosen territory owned by the player to their left (dead factions are not involved in this process)
  5. Mysterious Losses:
    Players don’t receive resources for this turn cycle.
  6. The End of an Age:
    Return to basic chaos rolls and all plague is removed from the map.
  7. Thieves in the Night:
    Players don’t receive gold for this turn cycle.
  8. Unexpected Treachery:
    Each player is forced to wipe all units from a single chosen territory owned by the player to their right (dead factions are not involved in this process).
  9. Something in the Water:
    For the rest of the game whenever a player attempts to cross a water route (invasion or fortification) with a unit they must roll a six sided die for that unit. If they roll a 4, 5, or 6 the unit has survived the journey and if they do not the unit will be consumed (the Ishimatsu Clan can reroll 1’s in this process). Everytime this chaos number is rolled all naval fleets will be destroyed, but these rolls are resolved before naval fleet battles.
  10. Shadow Rulers:
    All players swap seats with the player to their left taking control of that player's faction (dead factions are included in this process so that defeated players may re-enter the game).
  11. Eternal Shadow:
    For the rest of the game whenever a player attempts to move into any territory (invasion or fortification) with a unit they must roll a six sided die for that unit. If they roll a 4, 5, or 6 the unit has survived the journey and if they do not the unit will be consumed (the Ishimatsu Clan can reroll 1’s in this process). Everytime this chaos number is rolled after this point all wealth will be discarded (Eternal Shadow overrides Something in the Water and Something in the Darkness)
Age of Oblivion Chaos Rolls
DICE ROLL: (roll two dice)
  1. Apocalypse:
    All players lose the game and Trӧm is engulfed in flames.
  2. The Great Contagion:
    Half the units present in each territory are killed (round up).
  3. The Fall of Civilization:
    All structures are destroyed.
  4. The Wrath of the Gods:
    Meteors strike the earth and destroy two random territories. All structures and units are decimated and the territories become permanently impassable.
  5. The Attrition of Fear:
    All territories on the map lose one unit (The Fallen are not affected).
  6. The End of an Age:
    Return to basic chaos rolls and all plague is removed from the map.
  7. The Rising Dead:
    Fallen territories receive one extra unit and all unoccupied territories receive one Fallen unit.
  8. The Great Corruption:
    Plague spawns on all Fallen controlled territories (The Fallen are not affected by plague).
  9. Watchers in the Rubble:
    The Fallen destroy all constructed walls and patrol all wall borders with a garrison of two Fallen soldiers for the rest of the game. Losing an attacking soldier adds a defensive die to The Fallen patrol.
  10. Pilgrimage of the Dead:
    The Fallen convert all units in the territory with the most units to Fallen soldiers and move to a random territory via the most efficient path (roll two dice to break ties for the most efficient route).
  11. Empire of the Dead:
    The Fallen take over all capitals and absorb all units and structures within those territories.
